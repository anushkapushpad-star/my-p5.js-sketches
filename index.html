Wassily Kandinsky
Color Study
1913  ·  Watercolour & gouache on paper
Original · Städtische Galerie im Lenbachhaus, Munich

Squares with Concentric Circles is not a full painting but a colour study — an investigation into how combinations of hue and tone are perceived. For Kandinsky, colour was the soul of a picture. A synaesthete by nature, he could "hear colours" and "see sounds," spending his life codifying those sensations into the language of abstraction.
